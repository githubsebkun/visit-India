Jaipur[North India]the Pink city of India

Jaipur is the capital city of Rajasthan. Jaipur is extremely popular tourist destination in India.

Jaipur is the desert capital of India known as the Pink City thanks to its pink walls and old buildings of the bygone era. The most popular attractions of Jaipur are below

City Palace.

This was once owned by the richest royal family. Huge complex of courtyards, gardens and buildings blend Rajput and Mughal architecture. The peacock gate is stunning showing big craftsmanship. The gallery, museum inside the City Palace is not to be missed.

Amber Fort and Palace. This was once the original home of Rajput Royals and is beautifully situated in a hill top containing impressive traditional temples, palaces and paintings.

Nahargarh Fort, also known as Tiger Fort is situated on the Aravali Hills over the Jaipur City. Built to protect to the city in 1734, this fort offers spectacular views especially during sunset.

Galta Monkey Temple is a Holy Temple built between two granite hills with three sacred pools. The temple complex of Ramgopalji temple is colloquially known as Monkey temple (Galwar Bagh) in travel literature, due to the large tribe of monkeys who live here. The architecture is stunning though tourists should be careful when dealing with monkeys as they could attack if they are very hungry and see you with lots of food.

“Hawa Mahal”- Palace of the Winds. This is the most popular tourist attraction, we can say. Constructed in 1799, with five floors, beautifully carved windows and screens. The flow of the wind through the buildings gave the name. In ancient times the lady’s used to watch the events down, without being noticed.

Jaigarh Fort. The big Jaigarh Fort was built in 18 the century. It contains large

cannons and big forts. Very well preserved and it is one best chance to see the medieval India military structures. Amber Fort, its beautiful interiors and the military cannons which make a real fort is not to be missed.

Shopping. Don’t forget the opportunity to shop good Indian-saris, kurtha (tops), rings, bangles, gems, stones. Do bargain and get some help of your guides before you go for bargains.
