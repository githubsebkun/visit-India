New Delhi [central India] the capital city of India

Major places to visit in New Delhi

New Delhi is home to historic sites and museums.

India Gate Built in 1931 as the national monument commemorating the 90,000 soldires of the Indian Army who lost their life fighting for British in WWI and WWII.

Gandhi Smrithi is the location where Mahatma Gandhi the Father of Nation spent his last 144 days of his life and was assassinated on 30 January 1948. Rajghat is the place where Mahatma Gandhi was cremated.

Jantar Mantar built by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur consists of 13 architectural astronomy instruments. The primary purpose of the observatory was to compile

astronomical tables, and to predict the times and movements of the sun, moon and planets

Built in 1193 Qutub Minar is part of the ancient capital of the Tughlaq dynasty

Rashtrapati Bhavan is the official residence of the President of India and is the largest residence of any head of state in the world

Indian Parliament is one of the most magnificent buildings in the world built British architect Edwin Lutyens and Herber Baker in 1912-13. This consists of Upper House and Lower House under President of India.

The Lotus Temple which is the Bahai's house of worship built in 1986. It has drawn to its portals more than 70 million visitors, making it one of the most visited edifices in the world.

PS: The famous Tajmahal is only 4 hours train journey from New Delhi
