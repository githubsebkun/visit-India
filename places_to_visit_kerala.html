Major places to visit in Kerala

Kochi

Kochi which is known as Queen of Arabian Sea it has major airport and harbor

Kochi known as Cochin is the major port city in the west coast of Kerala. It is very well known as Queen of Arabian Sea as it has been a major port of business from ancient times. Kochi is very famous for its beauty and plenty of attractions. One of the major attractions is the fishing nets known as 'Cheenavala'. It has become the business capital of Kerala State with its recent developments with major roads, international airport, metro railway etc. Kochi is a famous tourist place too with plenty of major star hotels. Kochi ranks first in the total number of international and domestic tourist arrivals in Kerala. Kochi has been ranked the sixth best tourist destination in India according to a survey conducted by the Nielsen Company on behalf of the Outlook Traveler magazine. Kochi was one of the 28 Indian cities found to be among the emerging 440 global cities that will contribute 50% of the world GDP by the year 2025, in a study done by McKinsey Global Institute. It is advised to travel first to Kochi when you travel to Kerala as it helps to move to other places easily


Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala
Thiruvananthapuram also known as Trivandrum, is the capital of the Indian state of Kerala and the headquarters of the Thiruvananthapuram District. It is located on the west coast of India near the extreme south of the mainland. Referred to by Mahatma Gandhi as "Evergreen city of India", the city is characterized by its undulating terrain of low coastal hills and busy commercial alleys. The city is home to central and state government offices and organizations. Apart from being the political nerve center of Kerala, it is also a major academic hub and is home to several educational institutions including the University of Kerala, and to many science and technology institutions, the most prominent being the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC). A major milestone in the city's recent history was the establishment of Technopark—India's first IT park—in 1995. Technopark has developed into the largest IT park in India in geographical area, employing around 40,000 people in 300 companies. This placed Thiruvananthapuram on the IT map of India.


Thrissur

Thrissur is known as the cultural capital of Kerala

Thrissur is originally known as Thrissivaperoor. Thrissur is known as the cultural capital of Kerala. It is also a major hub for financial and commercial institutions. It is situated 75k.m north east of Kochi. It is also famous for its gold jewelery making industry. The city is also very famous for its Thrissur puram a major festival among two major temples. It is also a major hub for the ayurvedic treatments. Thrissur has played major role in the political parties in Kerala.

Thrissur Pooram is a popular temple festival of Kerala. It is held at the Vadakkunnathan Temple in Thrissur town every year. The golden elephant(Nettipattam), elephant accoutrements (Chamayam), ornamental fan made of peacock feathers (Aalavattom), royal fan (Venchamarom), sacred bells and decorative umbrellas are prepared and Pooram display usually have innovative patterns and varieties of fireworks display
